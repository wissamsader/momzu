A voice AI that lives on your Mac.

what it does
01

Hold space. Speak.

Press and hold space, say what you want, let go. Or hit ⌃⌥⌘ from anywhere on your Mac. A soft chime tells you it heard — then it thinks, and talks back. If it runs off with the wrong idea, say stop. It stops. Mid-sentence, mid-task, instantly. And when you'd rather write than talk, dictation mode types your words into any app.

two minutes of setup
  1. install — paste the line above into terminal, press enter. momzu downloads, installs, and opens itself. allow the microphone when it asks; first launch fetches its ears + natural voice (one time).
  2. connect a mind — momzu is the body; you pick the brain. using claude (recommended)? install claude code in terminal — curl -fsSL https://claude.ai/install.sh | bash — then run claude once and sign in with your claude account. otherwise press keys in momzu and paste a deepseek or gemini api key — or go fully offline with ollama.
  3. talk — hold space and speak, or press ⌃⌥⌘ from anywhere. a soft chime means it got you.
